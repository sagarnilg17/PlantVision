# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Casual plant parents — people with a handful of houseplants who keep forgetting to water them. Low botanical knowledge, high emotional attachment. They open the app when something looks off with a leaf, or when they can't remember if they watered yesterday. They want to feel like a competent caretaker, not like they're consulting a database.

## Product Purpose

Plant Care is an AI-powered plant companion that identifies plants from photos, diagnoses health issues in real time, and builds an adaptive care schedule adjusted to each plant's actual light environment. Success means someone who was previously anxious about keeping their plants alive now feels confident and in control of their garden.

## Positioning

The combination of AI identification + real-time health diagnosis + environment-aware care engine in a single scan flow. Not a reference app, not a reminder app — a system that understands your specific plant, in your specific space, and tells you exactly what it needs. Where PictureThis stops at the name, this continues to the care plan.

## Brand Personality

**Delightful · Smart · Minimal.** The interface should feel like texting a botanist friend who gives crisp, useful answers and makes you feel capable. Satisfying micro-interactions that reward real actions (a watering logged, an illustration generating), intelligent defaults, and nothing on screen that doesn't serve the user's next move. Warmth comes from precision and craft, not from softness and stock-photo leaves.

## Anti-references

- **PictureThis / iNaturalist** — dense scientific cards, information overwhelm, reference-tool UX. Users shouldn't feel like they opened Wikipedia.
- **Duolingo / gamified wellness apps** — streaks, XP bars, badges, hollow celebration mechanics. This is a tool for living things, not a habit tracker.
- **Generic "nature app" green** — stock-photo leaves on cream backgrounds, hand-drawn leaf icons, bubbly rounded-everything. The category reflex; avoid it entirely.
- **Cold AI / tech products** — dark mode with neon accents, robot-y iconography, developer-tool aesthetic. This lives on the same phone as someone's camera roll, not their terminal.

## Design Principles

**Show, don't burden.** Surface the one thing the user needs right now — the water button, the health status, the scan prompt. Every secondary detail should require an intentional tap to reach.

**Confidence through precision.** Vague guidance ("water regularly") erodes trust; specific guidance ("water in 5 days, adjusted for your low-light spot") builds it. The AI's value is in specificity, and the UI should reinforce that.

**Delight that earns its place.** Micro-interactions reward real actions, not arbitrary milestones. Motion is purposeful — it communicates state changes, not decoration.

**Familiar materials, unexpected craft.** The UI should feel native to the phone and elevated beyond the category — not clinical white, not nature-cliché. The differentiation is in the quality of execution, not the loudness of the branding.

**Live in the negative space.** The app is used in short, anxious bursts ("is my plant okay?"). Clarity over density; every screen should resolve in under 3 seconds of reading.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA throughout — minimum 4.5:1 contrast on body text, screen reader labels on all interactive elements, keyboard navigable flows. Reduced motion respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`.
